Birds

Birds, scientifically classified as class Aves, are warm-blooded vertebrates characterized by feathers, toothless beaked jaws, hard-shelled eggs, a high metabolic rate, a four-chambered heart, and a strong, yet lightweight skeleton. They are a diverse group, with over 11,000 living species, ranging in size from the bee hummingbird to the common ostrich.
